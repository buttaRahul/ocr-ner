Magical realism is a prominent genre of literature
that is defined by the presence of magical elements 
within a realistic worldly setting , thereby effectively
combining fantasy with reality . The overall concept
was mainly developed and popularized in latin
America where notalbe writers such as Isabelle
Allende and Gabriel Garcia Marquez utilized it
to comment upon the tumultouus socail and
political conditions of the region . It is mainly
characterized by a sense of heightered mystery
and general ambiguity concerning the nature of
Magic . In addition , the narrative is typically
geared towards addressing real-life issues . In
this manner , the stories act as allegorical and
symbolic critiques of #societal at affairs . Lastly ,
the literary works tend to move away from 
the typical plot structure with a clear exposition ,
climax and resolution .
